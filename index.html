How I'd build the CAB
at Anthropic.

CAB programs are typically a 4–6 month process from first conversation to launch. The 90-day framework below covers the foundation — audience mapping, infrastructure, recruitment, and operating design. The launch follows once the right members are in place and the program is built to last.

Days 1–30
Listen & Map
  • —Audit existing customer relationships, references, and advocacy assets across Sales, CS, and Product
  • —Interview internal stakeholders — CCO, CPO, field teams — to map what they need from a CAB that they don't have today
  • —Define the ideal CAB member profile: industry, title, company size, AI maturity, strategic value to Anthropic's roadmap
  • —Build the target named-account list — starting with existing customers best positioned to shape Anthropic's enterprise narrative
Days 31–60
Build & Recruit
  • —Design the CAB charter: purpose, member expectations, meeting cadence, confidentiality framework, and what Anthropic commits to in return
  • —Personally recruit founding members — C-suite and VP-level leaders from enterprise AI adopters across regulated industries
  • —Design the first CAB session agenda: executive-worthy, insight-driven, not a product pitch — with clear pre-work and post-session deliverables
  • —Stand up the operating infrastructure: intake forms, feedback loops, CRM tagging, and reporting templates
Days 61–90
Finalize & Prepare for Launch
  • —Finalize founding member roster — confirm participation, align on charter expectations, and complete onboarding for each executive
  • —Design the inaugural CAB session agenda: executive-worthy, insight-driven, not a product pitch — with clear pre-work and post-session deliverables
  • —Complete operating infrastructure: intake forms, feedback loops, CRM tagging, and reporting templates ready before day one
  • —Present 90-day readout to CCO and CPO — member list, program design, and launch plan for months 4–6
→
Months 4–6 · CAB Launch

The first 90 days lay the foundation. The launch — inaugural session, closed-loop VoC engine, and executive reporting — follows in months 4–6 once the right members are recruited, the charter is agreed, and the program is built to run. Rushing the launch is how CABs fail. Getting the foundation right is how they last.

The operating model.
How it runs after launch.

A CAB that meets twice a year and produces a summary PDF isn't a CAB — it's a focus group. Here's how I'd run it as a living, working program.

Membership Design
  • —15–25 founding members, C-suite and VP-level, across 4–6 industries where Anthropic has strategic concentration
  • —Segmented by AI maturity — early adopters, fast followers, and regulated-industry leaders — so insights represent the full enterprise spectrum
  • —Named executive sponsor from Anthropic's leadership paired to each member — making retention a shared accountability
Cadence & Format
  • —2 full-day in-person CAB sessions per year — structured around Anthropic's product roadmap moments, not the calendar
  • —Quarterly pulse check: 20-minute virtual touchpoint per member cohort to maintain momentum between summits
  • —Ad-hoc advisory moments: 1:1 calls when a major product decision needs real customer perspective before shipping
Insight → Action Loop
  • —Every CAB session produces a structured insight brief delivered to Product, GTM, and executive leadership within 5 business days
  • —Each insight is tagged, owner-assigned, and tracked to a decision — so members see their input reflected in outcomes, not just acknowledged
  • —"What we heard, what we did" report back to CAB members at every session — closing the loop is what keeps executives showing up
How I'll Report the Number
  • —Coverage: % of strategic named accounts represented in the CAB vs. total target universe
  • —Engagement: session attendance, pulse check participation, and 1:1 touchpoint frequency per member
  • —Influence: CAB-sourced insights tied to roadmap decisions, GTM pivots, and expansion pipeline — tracked to methodology I can stand behind
Why this works specifically for Anthropic

Anthropic's enterprise customers are navigating AI adoption in real time — often without a playbook. A CAB structured around AI maturity stages rather than just industry or company size gives Anthropic a unique insight engine: what does responsible AI adoption actually look like on the ground at a Fortune 500 bank vs. a healthcare system vs. a global logistics company? Those are the stories that feed the product roadmap, the analyst narrative, the GTM motion, and the executive keynote. That's the CAB I'd build.
